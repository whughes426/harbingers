### Background
Vestra “Ves” Val’tan grew up straddling the line between two worlds—technically, a Kryn Dynasty citizen but who was born and lives in the Dwendalian Empire—yet she has never felt she belonged to either. Her father, an ambassador of the Kryn Dynasty stationed in Rexxentrum, met her mother during his diplomatic service. Though their marriage is one of love, it came with challenges, particularly as they sought to raise Ves in a city where her drow heritage often drew suspicion. While deeply respected, her mother, being the Headmistress of the Soltryce Academy and a powerful Archmage, has had to navigate the suspicions and distrust her Empire colleagues have of her Dynasty husband. Her father’s relationship with the Dynasty is fraught with tension. Though loyal to his people, he opposes the more violent tactics the Dynasty employs in pursuit of its goals. This quiet rebellion has shaped Ves’s life, keeping her apart from the culture of her father’s homeland and its deep connection to the Luxon, the Dynasty’s revered deity. Protective of their only child, Ves’s parents ensured she was educated by the Cobalt Soul at the temple of Ioun, where the priestesses and scholars could teach her the virtues of knowledge, light, and discipline. 

You see, since childhood Ves has struggled to make sense of her frequent and intense nightmares, visions, and voices in her head. Her parents, concerned for her wellbeing, believed the temple of Ioun might help quiet the voices and guide her toward stability. While Ves thrived in many aspects of her clerical training, excelling in her studies and the arts of healing, her faith faltered. Prayer and meditation often felt hollow, and then a symbol appeared in her dreams and has captivated her in ways she cannot explain. She began sketching the symbol in secret, praying to it instead of Ioun, all the while wracked with guilt over what felt like a betrayal of the goddess she had been raised to revere. When her mentor discovered this, she didn’t reprimand Ves. Instead, she suggested Ves travel to Rosohna to seek out anyone who might provide answers about the mysterious symbol.

At 25, Ves left the temple and her family behind, embarking on a journey to uncover the truth about her identity. The decision to leave was a painful one; she cried the night she departed, knowing her parents would have tried to stop her had she told them. For Ves, this journey is not just about the visions—it’s about finding a place where she belongs, something that has always eluded her.

Her journey to Rosohna is one of both self-discovery and survival. She knows little of the Kryn Dynasty beyond her father’s cautious teachings, and her own doubts about destiny leave her unsure of what she will find there. She clings to her clerical training, even as she becomes more and more aware that other magical abilities (sorcerous abilities) have begun to manifest in her spells, complicating her understanding of herself further. Despite her doubts, and now traveling with a new-found friend, the goblin, Grimm, Ves’s determination drives her forward. She has no illusions of grandeur—she simply wants answers. Who is she? What are these visions, voices, and symbols? And why does she feel pulled toward a faith she does not understand?
